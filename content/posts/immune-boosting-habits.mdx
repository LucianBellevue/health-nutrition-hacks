## The immune basics (simple and repeatable)

- Sleep: consistent bedtime/wake time supports recovery [1]
- Nutrition: protein + colorful produce helps cover key nutrients [2]
- Hydration: being run down often feels worse when you’re under-hydrated [3]
- Stress: small daily calming habits help support resilience

Related reading: [Natural Supplements for Stress Relief](/blog/natural-supplements-for-stress-relief)

---

## Daily immune-support habits

&nbsp;

### 1) Prioritize consistent sleep

CDC shares practical sleep hygiene tips (timing, light, routine) that support better sleep consistency. [1]

&nbsp;

### 2) Eat protein at breakfast

Protein-forward mornings help support steady energy and better choices later. [2]

&nbsp;

### 3) Include 2 produce colors at lunch + dinner

A simple “color rule” increases nutrient variety.

<PostImage
  slug="immune-boosting-habits"
  src="section-1.webp"
  alt="Citrus, ginger, and tea ingredients laid out for a simple routine"
  variant="section"
/>

&nbsp;

### 4) Hydrate consistently

Harvard Health notes low fluids can make you feel tired and weaker than usual. [3]

&nbsp;

### 5) Add light daily movement

A short walk counts and supports overall health habits.

&nbsp;

### 6) Use supplements only when they match your needs

NIH ODS fact sheets are a good starting point for understanding vitamin D, vitamin C, and zinc. [4][5][6]
