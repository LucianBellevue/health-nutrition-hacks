## How to pick a stress supplement (without stacking 6 things)

Start with:

- one main goal (daytime calm, nighttime wind-down, or stress resilience)
- one supplement for 2–3 weeks
- one simple habit (walk, breathing, bedtime consistency)

Related reading: [Magnesium Glycinate Benefits](/blog/magnesium-glycinate-benefits)

## 6 natural supplements people use for stress support

&nbsp;

### 1) Magnesium glycinate (night routine)

NIH ODS notes magnesium supports muscle and nerve function and is required for energy production. [3]

&nbsp;

### 2) L-theanine (calm focus)

Often used for calm focus during the day.

&nbsp;

### 3) Ashwagandha (stress resilience)

NCCIH notes ashwagandha may be safe short-term (up to 3 months) but includes important cautions, including avoiding use during pregnancy and rare liver injury reports. [1]

&nbsp;

### 4) Rhodiola (stress-related fatigue)

NCCIH notes rhodiola is possibly safe short-term (up to 12 weeks) and lists possible side effects like insomnia and dizziness. [2]

&nbsp;

### 5) Lemon balm (gentle wind-down)

Often used as tea or capsules for evening routines.

&nbsp;

### 6) Chamomile tea (simple habit)

A routine-builder that pairs well with an earlier bedtime.

## Beginner mistakes to avoid

- starting 3 supplements at once
- taking rhodiola late in the day if it affects sleep [2]
- assuming “natural” means risk-free
- ignoring pregnancy and medication cautions [1][2][3]
