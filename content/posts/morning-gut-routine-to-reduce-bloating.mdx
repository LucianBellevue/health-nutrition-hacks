## Why mornings matter for digestion

Your gut is influenced by your sleep, hydration, stress levels, and how quickly you start eating. A calm, consistent morning routine can help support:

- regularity
- less “stuck” feeling
- fewer bloating triggers (like rushing meals or eating too fast)

## Morning gut routine: 7 simple steps

&nbsp;

### 1) Drink water first (before coffee)

Start with a full glass of water. This is the simplest “gut habit” that most people skip.

&nbsp;

### 2) Add lemon (optional) and sip slowly

Lemon water isn’t magic—but it can make water easier to drink consistently.

<PostImage
  slug="morning-gut-routine"
  src="section-1.jpg"
  alt="Glass of lemon water beside supplements on a bright kitchen counter"
  variant="section"
/>

&nbsp;

### 3) Wait 10–30 minutes before your first big meal

If you wake up and immediately eat a large meal, some people feel heavier/bloatier. Try giving your body a little time to wake up first.

&nbsp;

### 4) Gentle movement for 3–10 minutes

Light movement helps “wake up” your core and can support regularity.

- easy walk
- light stretching
- a few gentle yoga moves

&nbsp;

### 5) Eat a gut-friendly breakfast (protein + fiber)

Aim for:

- **protein** (eggs, Greek yogurt, protein smoothie)
- **fiber** (berries, oats, chia, flax)

&nbsp;

### 6) Add a probiotic (if it fits your routine)

Probiotics can be a helpful routine add-on for some people, especially when used consistently.

## What to avoid in the morning (common bloat triggers)

- Coffee on an empty stomach (if it irritates you)
- Eating too fast or while stressed
- Skipping water, then “making up for it” later
- Huge breakfasts after not eating much the day before
- Trying 3 new supplements at once
